What Is Business Process Documentation?

Business process documentation involves comprehensively describing the steps to carry out a task. Typically, this practice includes compiling various documents, such as policies, tutorials, and checklists.
Most importantly, process documentation entails reviewing the business's procedures and creating a process map. Process mapping allows managers to visually see how the different steps in the task are connected, as well as its outputs and inputs.

  • TEN STEPS TO PROCESS DOCUMENTATION

  • Process Name

    The documentation process begins by writing down the process name and a concise description of its purpose.

  • Process Boundaries

    The process's start and endpoints need to be determined, as well as what prompts it to begin. Executives should also identify what factors demonstrate that the process is completed.

  • Process Outputs

    Next, identify the output that the process produces.

  • Process Inputs

    The team will then list all the supplies and raw materials they need to conduct the process and where they can source them. Some common inputs are paper,software and fabric.

  • Process Activities

    At this stage, individuals in the meeting should make a note of all the activities that need to be included in the process. For example, to produce a new children's toy, the brand will need to submit a trademark request and create supplier orders.

  • Process Organization

    Once all process activities are identified, the team should collaborate and put them into a cohesive sequence. It may be helpful to record the reason why a task is at a specific step for future reference.

  • Process Review

    It is important to review the sequence in its entirety after it is organized. Teams should make sure the process is complete according to the established boundaries from Step 2.

  • Process Roles

    Afterward, identify who is responsible for performing process activities and assign different roles for each step. For instance, a procurement manager will be in charge of creating purchase orders, while a quality control team will inspect the delivery.

  • Transcribe Process

    The process should then be recorded from the whiteboard onto a physical or online document.

  • Final Process Review

    Finally, all team members in the meeting must review and approve the process flow.
